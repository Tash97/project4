Yellowstone National Park is a vast wilderness area located primarily in the U.S. state of Wyoming, but also extending into Montana and Idaho. The park was established in 1872 as the first national park in the world, and it remains one of the most popular and iconic natural areas in the United States. Yellowstone is known for its stunning landscapes, diverse wildlife, and geothermal features such as hot springs and geysers.

The park covers an area of over 2 million acres, and it is home to a wide variety of ecosystems, from high mountain peaks and dense forests to vast grasslands and thermal basins. Visitors to Yellowstone can explore the park by hiking, camping, fishing, boating, and wildlife watching. Some of the park's most popular attractions include the Old Faithful geyser, the Grand Canyon of Yellowstone, and the Lamar Valley, which is known for its abundant wildlife.

Yellowstone is also home to a diverse array of wildlife, including grizzly bears, wolves, elk, bison, and many other species. The park is a vital part of efforts to protect and conserve these animals, as well as their habitats. Visitors are encouraged to respect the park's wildlife and follow guidelines to ensure their safety.

Whether you're interested in outdoor adventure, natural beauty, or wildlife conservation, Yellowstone National Park is an incredible destination that offers something for everyone.
